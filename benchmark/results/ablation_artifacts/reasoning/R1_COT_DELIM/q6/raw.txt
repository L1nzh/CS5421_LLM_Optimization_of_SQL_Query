### Performance Bottleneck Analysis
1. **Correlated subquery for item average price**: Runs once per `item` row, leading to O(n) repeated expensive calculations on the `item` table.
2. **Implicit comma joins**: Less readable and increases risk of accidental predicate misalignment, though functionally equivalent to explicit joins.
3. **Repeated evaluation of uncorrelated `d_month_seq` subquery**: While PostgreSQL may cache it, precomputing it once eliminates redundant execution.

### Rewrite Steps
1. Replace implicit comma joins with explicit `INNER JOIN` syntax for clarity and consistent planner behavior.
2. Precompute the target `d_month_seq` once in a CTE to avoid repeated evaluation.
3. Replace the correlated average price subquery with a pre-aggregated CTE that calculates average price per category once, then join to the `item` table to eliminate row-by-row subquery execution.
4. Push all filters close to base tables to reduce row counts early in the execution pipeline.

<SQL>
WITH target_month AS (
    SELECT DISTINCT d_month_seq AS seq
    FROM date_dim
    WHERE d_year = 2000 AND d_moy = 1
),
category_avg_price AS (
    SELECT i_category, AVG(i_current_price) AS avg_price
    FROM item
    GROUP BY i_category
)
SELECT
  a.ca_state state,
  count(*) cnt
FROM customer_address a
INNER JOIN customer c
  ON a.ca_address_sk = c.c_current_addr_sk
INNER JOIN store_sales s
  ON c.c_customer_sk = s.ss_customer_sk
INNER JOIN date_dim d
  ON s.ss_sold_date_sk = d.d_date_sk
INNER JOIN target_month tm
  ON d.d_month_seq = tm.seq
INNER JOIN item i
  ON s.ss_item_sk = i.i_item_sk
INNER JOIN category_avg_price cap
  ON i.i_category = cap.i_category
WHERE i.i_current_price > 1.2 * cap.avg_price
GROUP BY a.ca_state
HAVING count(*) >= 10
ORDER BY cnt
LIMIT 100;
</SQL>
